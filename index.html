Shuan [ʃwan] Osman Karim

I am a Kurdish American Linguist

My research focuses primarily on the diachronic study of the Iranian languages, focusing on those languages spoken in the Kurdish region. Additionally, I am interested in synchronic topics, including inferential-realizational morphology, categorial grammars (syntax), and language documentation. My innate love of language has brought me to study and work with many languages outside my Iranian focus, including Sanskrit, Japanese, Spanish, Akkadian, Syriac, Old Church Slavonic, and more.

My Background

About me

I am an Alexander von Humboldt postdoctoral fellow at Julius-Maximilians-Universität Würzburg (JMU). I have a Ph.D. in linguistics at Ohio State University. My research interests are historical/diachronic linguistics, morphology, and language documentation, with an areal focus on Iranian languages. In an interview conducted for the LSA's linguistics StoryCorps, I heard someone remark, ``if you get to know a language, it will tell you its problems.'' This quote serves to underline my research philosophy. I believe that quality research develops from a deep understanding of languages and the communities that speak them, coupled with strong linguistic fundamentals. This belief influences the way I teach linguistics, integrating my students' diverse linguistic backgrounds into the teaching of core concepts, and it influences how I conduct research, constantly learning and studying diverse languages, advancing them steadily descriptively and theoretically.

Current Research Focus

I am currently working on the diachronic reconstruction of earlier stages of Gorani, Zazaki, and Kurdish. My approach differs from most works in Iranian historical linguistics by rejecting the top-down approach. I employ the "Neo-Grammarian" Comparative Method using data collected from fieldwork from current speakers of modern varieties. My goal is to reconsruct earlier stages of these subfamilies with the same level rigour and acurracy as scholars were able to achieve in the reconstruction of Proto-Indo-European.
